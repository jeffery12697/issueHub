## Design Context

### Users
Software developers and cross-functional teams (devs, designers, PMs) tracking tasks, bugs, and sprints. They spend long sessions inside the app — they need fast navigation, clear hierarchy, and zero cognitive overhead. Inspired by Linear, ClickUp, and Jira.

### Brand Personality
Focused · Fast · Clean — a productivity tool that gets out of the way. The interface should feel confident and sharp, not flashy. Users should feel in control.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Visual tone**: Minimal and sharp, leaning Linear — tight spacing, white/slate-50 surfaces, violet as the single accent color
- **Primary accent**: `violet-600` (buttons, active states, focus rings, links)
- **Neutrals**: Slate palette — backgrounds `slate-50`, borders `slate-200`, muted text `slate-400/500`
- **Typography**: Currently system-ui default; could benefit from a geometric sans (Inter or Geist) for sharper headings
- **Radius**: `rounded-xl` for cards, `rounded-lg` for buttons/inputs — consistent softness without being bubbly
- **Shadows**: Minimal (`shadow-sm`) — elevation expressed through borders, not heavy shadows
- **No dark mode** currently; light-only
- **Anti-references**: Avoid generic SaaS purple gradients, nested card-in-card patterns, Inter everywhere without intentional typographic hierarchy

### Design Principles
1. **Clarity over decoration** — every visual element must serve information hierarchy; remove anything that doesn't help the user make a decision
2. **Violet is a signal, not wallpaper** — use `violet-600` only for interactive, active, or actionable states; never as background decoration
3. **Density with breathing room** — task-tracker users want information density; use compact rows and tight spacing but preserve whitespace at section and card level
4. **Consistent motion** — transitions are `150–200ms ease` only; no bouncing, no elastic; reduced motion must always be respected
5. **Status speaks first** — priority colors (sky/amber/orange/red) and status badges are the highest-priority visual signals on any task surface; keep them legible at a glance
